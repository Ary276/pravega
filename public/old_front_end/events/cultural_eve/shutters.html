Aperture

Aperture is the opening in the lens, similar to the pupil in the eye. It is measured in f/numbers or f-stops. High f-stop numbers like f/16 or f/22 mean small aperture size or narrow aperture and low f-stop numbers like f/1.4 or f/2 mean large or wide apertures.

Aperture affects two things, brightness/exposure and depth of field.

Larger apertures let more light in and naturally need to be compensated by having fast shutter speeds or low ISO, and vice versa.

Depth of field as explained in the last post, is how selective the focus range is. Wide apertures, giving shallow depth of field, are preferred for portrait shots, while narrow apertures with large depth of field are more suited to landscape photos.
