Decoherence


Come forward and show your prowess in Physics by competing with students across the country to solve problems through deliberation and critical thinking. Prepare for a battle to the end.

This is gonna be intense.
This. Is. Decoherence.

This is an unique opportunity for any physics enthusiast to showcase their problem solving ability and insight in the subject. The prelims is knocking at the door and this will serve as a gateway to the main Decoherence event!

The preliminary round is divided into two rounds - one objective and one subjective. The objective round will serve as a screening round to select teams for the subjective round. We promise that these two rounds themselves will put your abilities to test!

Unleash your skills in the Prelims to secure your way into the main event! The event will be full of fun for those who love teasing their minds with new Physics problems, with participants getting the opportunity to present their solutions to leading Physicists in the panel of judges!

Sounds interesting? Then don't miss the prelims.

Dates


  • Objective: 10 to 11:30 AM IST, 13th June, 2021
  • Subjective: 20th June, 2021
  • Finals: TBD. During Pravega '21(July 29 - August 1)


Eligibility


High school students to 3rd year UG. Contestants can participate in teams of upto 2 members.
Prizes worth 40k, including prizes to winners and few best solution awards. All finalists and award winners will also recieve certificates.



Rules and Regulations


Note: 'Team' here refers to both, a team of 2 participants and a single participant.

Objective prelims:
  • 30 questions (20 single option correct type and 10 multiple option correct type)
  • Duration: 90 minutes including submission time
  • No late submission allowed.
  • 25 teams will be selected for subjective prelims.
  • For more details (regarding submission procedure, handling of ties, network and other issues etc), refer to rules and regulations document.

Subjective prelims:
  • 8 questions - 6 theoretical and 2 data analysis type
  • Duration: 18-24 hours. Will be informed well before the round.
  • 10 teams will be selected for finals.
  • For more details (regarding submission procesure, rules regarding extension of time etc) refer to rules and regulations document.

Finals:
  • 2 day event. Final plan will be uploaded later.
    • (Tentative plan)
  • Day 1 - Question followed by presentation by the teams in front of judges.
  • Day 2 - Theoretical, computational, experimental design related analysis of one or few questions.
  • For more details, refer to the rules and regulations document.
